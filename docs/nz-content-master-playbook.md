## 3. INSTAGRAM ALGORITHM — HOW IT WORKS (2025/2026)

Instagram has **separate ranking systems** for Feed, Reels, Stories, and Explore.

### Universal Ranking Signals:
- **User Activity** — what they like, comment, save, share, watch
- **Relationship Strength** — DMs, comments, story replies, profile visits
- **Engagement Velocity** — how fast a post gets engagement after publishing
- **Recency** — newer posts favoured
- **Originality** — reposted content (especially with watermarks) is actively penalised. 10+ reposts in 30 days = excluded from Reels/Explore
- **Watch Time & Retention** — how long people spend on your content
- **DM Shares (Sends per Reach)** — THE #1 signal for Reels distribution to non-followers

### Reels Algorithm (discovery engine):
- Designed to reach **non-followers** (your main growth lever)
- **Retention-driven**: watch time, completion rate, replays are critical
- **DM shares** = most impactful signal for unconnected reach
- **Saves** = strong interest signal
- **Cold-start testing phase**: Instagram tests your Reel with a small audience first — early performance determines wider distribution
- **Trial Reels** (for 1K+ followers): publish to non-followers first to test before showing to your audience
- **Sweet spot length**: 7–15 seconds for optimal loop/replay rates (up to 3 min allowed)
- **Trending audio** still gives a boost

### Feed Algorithm (relationship engine):
- **Relationship strength** is the #1 factor
- **Interest prediction** based on past behaviour
- **Recency** matters
- **Conversation depth** > comment count
- **Keywords/SEO** in captions now more effective than hashtags for discovery

### What Instagram Penalises:
- Reposted content with watermarks
- Engagement bait ("Like this if you agree!")
- Low-quality or blurry media
- Hashtag stuffing (more than 5)
- Clickbait that doesn't deliver

## 4. FACEBOOK ALGORITHM — HOW IT WORKS (2025/2026)

### The 4-Step Ranking Process:
1. **Inventory** — gathers all available posts (friends, pages, groups, recommendations)
2. **Signals** — analyses hundreds of thousands of signals per post
3. **Predictions** — AI models predict likelihood of engagement (Meta's "Andromeda" AI considers dwell time, sentiment analysis, intent signals)
4. **Relevance Score** — higher score = higher placement in feed

### Key Facts:
- **50%+ of feed content** now comes from accounts users DON'T follow (AI recommendations)
- **Reels dominance** — all videos now classified as Reels; short-form vertical is the primary discovery engine
- **Same-day content** gets 50% more distribution
- **Private shares** (Messenger, WhatsApp) are heavily weighted
- **80/20 rule** — 80% value content, 20% promotional
- **First 60 minutes** after posting are critical (algorithm tests reception)

### What Facebook Favours:
- Original, high-quality content
- Reels (15–30 seconds, vertical, hook in first 2–3 seconds)
- Posts that spark genuine conversations
- Saves, shares, and comments (not just likes)
- Consistent posting (4–5 feed posts/week median)
- Burned-in captions on video

### What Facebook Penalises:
- Engagement bait
- Clickbait
- Low-effort reposts
- Overly promotional content
- External links in initial posts (put links in comments instead)

## 5. CAPTION WRITING — THE HOOK FORMULA

### The #1 Rule: First 1–2 Lines = Everything
The hook determines if someone taps "See More" or keeps scrolling.

### Proven Hook Patterns:

**Question Hook** (37% more comments on average):
> "Still wondering why your [X] isn't working?"
> "What if I told you there's one thing most people miss about [topic]?"

**Curiosity Gap Hook:**
> "There's one thing most [audience] never check — and it's costing them [result]."
> "I have a confession to make…"

**Bold Statement Hook:**
> "Here's what no one tells you about [topic]…"
> "Stop doing [common mistake] — here's why."

**Bold Stat Hook:**
> "95% of [audience] are doing this wrong."
> "This one change increased [metric] by [X]%."

**Before/After Hook:**
> "6 months ago I was [problem]. Today I'm [result]."

**Problem-Agitation Hook:**
> "Struggling with [pain point]? You're not alone — but here's the fix."

**Direct Value Hook:**
> "X things you can start today to [goal]"
> "Save this for later — you'll need it."

**Relatable/Humour Hook:**
> "POV: you finally [relatable scenario]"
> "That moment when [universal experience]"

### Caption Structure:

```
[HOOK — 1-2 lines that stop the scroll]

[BODY — deliver on the promise: story, value, insight, tips]

[CTA — tell them what to do next]
```

### Caption Length Guidelines:
- **Short** (<150 chars): quick engagement, reactions, simple posts
- **Medium** (150–300 words): storytelling, behind-the-scenes
- **Long** (700–2,200 chars): educational, value-packed, "save-worthy" content

### Caption Best Practices:
- **Authenticity > Polish** — raw, genuine content outperforms over-produced posts
- **Tell mini-stories** — people are drawn to narratives
- **Use emojis strategically** — break up text, add visual appeal
- **Line breaks for readability** — nobody reads a wall of text
- **Keywords matter** — Instagram indexes captions for search (SEO)
- **Ask questions** — drives comments
- **CTAs**: "Save this", "Share with someone who needs this", "Drop a 🔥 if you agree", "Tag someone who…", "Which spot would YOU visit?"

### Caption Template for NZ Content:

> [HOOK — curiosity/bold claim about the location]
>
> [STORY — 2-4 lines describing the journey/experience, name specific places]
>
> [CTA — question or share prompt]
>
> [5 HASHTAGS — see hashtag strategy below]

**Example:**
> "I tried to visit every iconic spot in Queenstown in 10 seconds. Scene 5 broke me. 😅
>
> From the bungy bridge to the Remarkables, jet boats to Fergburger — this city hits different.
>
> Which one would YOU stop at? 👇
>
> #queenstown #omniflash #newzealand #hyperlapse #travelreel"

## 6. HASHTAG STRATEGY — THE 5-HASHTAG RULE

### The New Reality:
Instagram now **enforces a maximum of 5 hashtags** per post/Reel. Exceeding this = suppressed distribution in Explore, hashtag pages, and Reels recommendations.

### How to Maximise 5 Hashtags:

Each hashtag must earn its spot. Use this mix:

| Slot | Type | Purpose | Example |
|------|------|---------|---------|
| 1 | **Branded** | Unique to you | #michaelnettonz #genaidigitalnz |
| 2 | **Location** | Local discovery | #queenstownnz #christchurchnz |
| 3 | **Community** | Like-minded people | #nztravel #explorenz |
| 4 | **Product/Service** | What you offer | #omniflash #aivideo |
| 5 | **Campaign/Topic** | Content theme | #hyperlapse #travelreel |

### Rules:
- ✅ Niche > Broad — mid-tier tags (10K–500K posts) outperform mega-tags
- ✅ Rotate per post — don't use the same 5 every time
- ✅ Place in caption — Instagram now prefers hashtags in the original caption, NOT in the first comment
- ✅ Research — use Instagram search to check hashtag popularity
- ❌ More than 5 hashtags (distribution penalty)
- ❌ Generic tags (#instagood, #love, #fyp)
- ❌ Irrelevant trending tags
- ❌ Banned/spammy hashtags

### Beyond Hashtags:
Instagram's discovery now relies MORE on:
- **Caption keywords/SEO** — descriptive captions with natural keywords
- **On-screen text in Reels**
- **Engagement signals** (saves, shares, watch time)
- Hashtags are a **supporting signal**, not the primary discovery engine anymore

## 7. ENGAGEMENT BOOSTERS — WHAT MOST PEOPLE MISS

### The "Sends per Reach" Metric:
DM shares are now the **#1 signal** for both Instagram Reels and Facebook distribution. Create content people want to SEND to friends — more valuable than likes or even comments.

**What makes people send:**
- "I need to go there" — destinations they want to visit
- "That's MY city" — local pride content
- "How did they do that?" — impressive technique
- "Look at this" — visually stunning moments

### Save-Worthy Content:
Saves signal high value. Create content people bookmark:
- Tips/checklists ("8 Queenstown spots in 10 seconds")
- "How to" guides
- Reference content (locations, itineraries)

### Comment Depth > Comment Count:
The algorithm values **meaningful conversations** over raw comment numbers. Ask questions that spark genuine discussion, not just emoji responses.

### First 60 Minutes Are Critical:
Both platforms test your content with a small audience first. After posting:
- Be active (respond to comments immediately)
- Share to Stories
- Engage with other accounts to drive traffic back

### Avoid External Links in Posts:
On both platforms, posts with external links get reduced reach. Put links in comments or use "link in bio" CTAs.

### Originality Is Non-Negotiable:
Both platforms penalise:
- Reposted content (especially with TikTok watermarks)
- Low-effort recycled content
- Stock-heavy content with minimal editing

## 11. TOURISM VIDEO AD — BEST PRACTICES

### The Hook (First 1-3 Seconds)

| Hook Type | Example | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **Curiosity Gap** | "You won't believe what's behind door #5 in Christchurch..." | Creates information gap the brain needs to fill |
| **Visual Disruption** | Open with the most extreme/unexpected shot first | Stops the scroll through visual shock |
| **Challenge/Claim** | "8 spots. 10 seconds. One city. Let's go." | Sets expectation and promises payoff |

### The "What's Next" Factor (Scene-to-Scene Tension)
Each scene must do TWO things:
1. **Deliver a mini-wow** (current scene is satisfying)
2. **Create a pull** (need to see the next one)

**Three techniques:**

**Escalation:** Each scene gets progressively more impressive.
> Scene 1: Peaceful → Scene 2: Energetic → Scene 3: Visual overload → Scene 4: Jaw-drop → Scene 5: Maximum wow

**Contrast Juxtaposition:** Each scene dramatically contrasts the last.
> Snow → Beach → Underwater → Mountain peak (whiplash keeps eyes locked)

**Action Continuity:** End each scene mid-action — brain needs resolution.
> Jumping → cut mid-fall → Splashing → cut mid-splash → Biting → cut mid-bite (the cut happens BEFORE the satisfying moment)

### The Wow Factor (What Makes It Shareable)

| Trigger | Application |
|---|---|
| "I need to go there" | Show destination in a way they've never seen — not the brochure angle |
| "How did they do that?" | The AI hyper-lapse IS the wow — lean into it |
| "That's MY city" | NZ pride — locals share content showing their home looking epic |
| "I didn't know that existed" | Hidden gems > famous spots |

### Tourism Ad Framework:
```
HOOK (0-1s)    → Visual disruption or bold claim
PULL (1-3s)    → "Here's what you're about to see..."
PAYOFF (3-8s)  → Rapid-fire escalating scenes
TWIST (8-9s)   → Unexpected final shot (jump scare, reveal, laugh)
CTA (9-10s)    → "Share this with someone who needs to visit NZ"
```

## 13. OMNI FLASH — PROMPT ENGINEERING

### What Omni Flash Does Best:

| Strength | How to Capitalise |
|---|---|
| **Physics realism** | Describe physical actions in detail — "water spray arcs behind the jet boat as it spins" |
| **Natural motion** | "The subject walks confidently toward camera, hair blowing in the wind" |
| **Multimodal reasoning** | Feed reference image of yourself + text prompt = consistent you-in-frame |
| **Conversational editing** | Generate base → refine → polish. Don't try perfect in one prompt |
| **Real-world knowledge** | Just name the location — model fills in detail |
| **Lighting realism** | Describe specific lighting conditions |

### The 5-Element Prompt Framework (Google Official):

| Element | Purpose | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Goal** | What you're creating and for whom | "A fast-paced 10-second tourism reel for Instagram showcasing Queenstown" |
| **2. Input Role** | How to use uploaded assets | "Use the reference image as the main subject's face — maintain throughout all scenes" |
| **3. Scene** | Subject, setting, lighting, style | "Golden hour light on Lake Wakatipu, snow-capped Remarkables, cinematic colour grading" |
| **4. Motion** | Camera AND subject movement | "Dolly zoom into face, then whip-pan to reveal mountain panorama" |
| **5. Constraints** | What to avoid or lock | "Maintain subject appearance. No text overlays. No logos. Natural physics." |

### Camera Vocabulary Cheat Sheet:

| Term | Effect | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Hyperlapse** | Time-lapse with camera movement | Signature format — every scene transition |
| **Dolly zoom** | Camera moves while zooming = tension | Dramatic reveals (summit, stadium) |
| **Whip-pan** | Fast camera swing | Scene-to-scene transitions |
| **Tracking shot** | Camera follows subject | Walking/running scenes |
| **Low-angle shot** | Camera below, looking up | Makes subject look powerful |
| **Aerial drone shot** | Bird's eye | Landscape reveals, city overviews |
| **Close-up** | Tight on face/object | Emotional moments, food shots |
| **Wide establishing shot** | Full environment | Opening shots, location reveals |
| **Handheld movement** | Slight shake, organic | Action scenes, adventure energy |
| **Slow-motion** | Time stretched | Key action moments (bungy, splash) |

### Multi-Turn Refinement Strategy:

**Turn 1 — Base Shot:**
> "A man standing on Kawarau Bridge in Queenstown, New Zealand, looking down at the turquoise river below. Cinematic wide shot, golden hour lighting, snow-capped mountains in background."

**Turn 2 — Lock Identity:**
> "Keep the same man. Now he's jumping off the bungy platform, arms spread wide. Camera follows him in slow-motion as he falls toward the river."

**Turn 3 — Refine Physics:**
> "Add realistic bungy cord tension — the subject bounces back up naturally. Water spray from the river below. Camera rotates around him during the bounce."

**Turn 4 — Polish:**
> "Add cinematic colour grading — teal and orange tones. Subtle lens flare from the sun behind the mountains. Film grain texture."

### Prompt Template for NZ Hyper-Lapse Content:

```
[SHOT TYPE] of [SUBJECT/ACTION] at [LOCATION], [TIME OF DAY].
[PHYSICAL DETAIL — what's happening physically].
[CAMERA MOVEMENT — how the camera moves].
[STYLE/MOOD — colour, lighting, texture].
[TRANSITION HINT — what leads to the next scene].
```

### Key Prompt Tips:
- **Be specific, not vague:** "Golden hour light reflecting off lake surface" > "nice lighting"
- **Describe physics explicitly:** Water spray, hair movement, cloth rippling, cord tension
- **Name real locations:** The model knows NZ landmarks — use them
- **Use positive framing:** "Include only real data" > "Don't add fake elements"
- **Start broad, refine narrow:** First prompt = overview. Subsequent prompts = details
- **Lock the subject:** Always say "maintain the same person throughout" when using reference images
