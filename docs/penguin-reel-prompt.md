## FIRST 10s — "The Giant & The Photographer"

**[Attach: reference photo of Michael]**

Create a cinematic 10-second tourism reel for Instagram, vertical 9:16 format. Use the reference image as the main subject — maintain his face and appearance throughout. All scenes are set on the Auckland Islands, New Zealand sub-Antarctic. No other locations.

**Subject:** Michael, aged 48, Indian origin (Kerala), living in Christchurch, New Zealand. He wears a weatherproof olive jacket, tan beanie, and glasses/spectacles throughout. He carries a camera with a long lens. His look is consistent — same person, same visual signature, different moments. He is a wildlife photographer witnessing something most humans never see, not a model performing for camera.

**Color Bible:** Harsh sub-Antarctic palette — cold grey-green tussock, dark teal ocean, overcast silver sky, rain-washed rocks. The elephant seal is massive brown-grey skin, wet, imposing. The penguins are tiny black-white-blue against the seal's bulk. Michael's olive jacket and tan beanie are the only warm tones in frame — he pops against the cold world. Golden light appears only in the final scene (the photo reveal) — a hint of warmth in the cold. No filters. Raw, wild, real.

**Emotional Arc:** Shock (0-1.7s) → Discovery (1.7-3.3s) → Tension (3.3-5s) → Comedy (5-6.7s) → Scale (6.7-8.3s) → Pride (8.3-10s)

**Scene 1 (0–1.7s) — HOOK:**
Wide establishing shot. A massive elephant seal (4 meters, 4000kg) raises its head from the tussock and ROARS. Its mouth is open, breath visible in the cold air. In the foreground, three Little Blue Penguins (25cm each) scatter in panic. The size contrast is absurd — the seal's head alone is bigger than all three penguins combined. Camera holds still, letting the scale speak. This is the shot that stops the scroll.

**Scene 2 (1.7–3.3s) — THE PHOTOGRAPHER:**
Cut to: Michael crouched behind a rock, camera raised to his eye, long lens pointed at the seal. His eyes are wide above the viewfinder. He's witnessing something most humans never see. He presses the shutter — click. Camera tracks in slowly toward his face. The cold wind lifts the edge of his beanie. He doesn't move. He's locked in.

**Scene 3 (3.3–5s) — THE STAND-OFF:**
Medium shot. A Yellow-eyed Penguin (65cm) stands its ground in the middle of the frame. Behind it, the elephant seal looms, massive. Penguin vs seal. David vs Goliath. Neither moves. The penguin's pale yellow eyes stare forward. The seal's nostrils flare. Camera holds the tension — then the penguin takes one deliberate step forward. The seal blinks first. Camera tracks the penguin's step.

**Scene 4 (5–6.7s) — THE RETREAT:**
Tracking shot. A group of Little Blue Penguins waddle away from the seal in a chaotic line. One trips on a rock, rolls sideways, recovers, keeps going. Another bumps into a third. Pure Rico energy. Michael watches from behind his camera, the corner of his mouth twitching — he's trying not to laugh. Camera follows the penguins' retreat.

**Scene 5 (6.7–8.3s) — THE GIANT:**
Wide shot. The elephant seal lies on the beach, its massive body stretched across the foreground — wet brown-grey skin, scars, power. In the background, tiny penguins watch from a safe distance, lined up on the rocks like an audience. The size contrast is cinematic — 4000kg vs 1kg. The seal yawns. The penguins don't move. Camera slowly pulls back to reveal the full scale.

**Scene 6 (8.3–10s) — THE PHOTO:**
Close-up of Michael's camera screen. The photo he captured is displayed — the exact moment the seal scolded the penguins, the penguins scattering, the seal roaring. It's the award-winning shot. Michael smiles — quiet, proud, "I can't believe I got that." Camera pulls back to reveal Michael sitting on the tussock, camera in his lap, the seal and penguins coexisting behind him. Golden light breaks through the clouds for the first time — warmth in the cold. This is the frame people screenshot and share.

**Technical Notes:**
- Michael's olive jacket, tan beanie, and glasses remain consistent throughout
- The elephant seal must look physically massive — describe its weight, skin texture, breath in cold air
- Penguin behavior must look natural — no staged interactions
- Smooth transitions between scenes — use motion blur or whip-pan bridges
- Maintain consistent lighting: overcast grey (scenes 1-5) → golden breakthrough (scene 6)
- Subtle lens flares acceptable in scene 6 only
- No text overlays, no dialogue, no filters — clean cinematic grade only
- All locations are on the Auckland Islands, New Zealand sub-Antarctic

## SECOND 10s — "The Encounter"

**[Attach: reference photo of Michael]**

Create a cinematic 10-second tourism reel for Instagram, vertical 9:16 format. Use the reference image as the main subject — maintain his face and appearance throughout. All scenes are set in the South Island of New Zealand. No other locations.

**Subject:** Michael, aged 48, Indian origin (Kerala), living in Christchurch, New Zealand. He wears a cap or hat suited to each scene and glasses/spectacles throughout. His look is consistent — same person, same visual signature, different moments. He is a real person encountering wild penguins in their natural habitat, not a model performing for camera.

**Color Bible:** Dominant palette is cool ocean teal, dark navy, misty grey-green (Fiordland), and slate blue. Penguin bodies are black, white, and slate blue (Little Blue) with golden-yellow eyes and head markings (Yellow-eyed, Fiordland Crested). Michael wears warm-toned clothing (olive, tan, burnt orange) to contrast the cool penguin world. Cool environment, warm subject — he pops against the backdrop. Sunset scenes at Oamaru add golden-orange warmth on penguin backs and Michael's skin. No filters. Clean, natural, wild.

**Emotional Arc:** Hook (0-1.2s) → Tension (1.2-2.4s) → Comedy (2.4-4.8s) → Drama (4.8-7.2s) → Payoff (7.2-10s)

**Scene 1 (0–1.2s) — HOOK:**
Close-up of a Yellow-eyed Penguin's face, staring directly into camera. Pale yellow eyes, unblinking, golden-yellow eye band visible. Background is blurred green bush. The penguin owns this frame. This is the rarest penguin on Earth, and it's looking at you like you're the endangered one. Camera holds still. No movement. Just eye contact.

**Scene 2 (1.2–2.4s) — STARE-DOWN:**
Cut to: Michael's face, same expression, staring back at the penguin. Same framing, same eye-line. He's wearing a beanie and glasses. He blinks first. A tiny smile cracks. He lost. Camera holds on his reaction.

**Scene 3 (2.4–3.6s) — CHAOS:**
A Little Blue Penguin (25cm tall, the world's smallest) trips on a rock on the Oamaru beach, rolls sideways, recovers, keeps walking like nothing happened. Michael crouches in the background, hand over his mouth, trying not to laugh. Camera tracks the penguin's waddle. Golden sunset light on the penguin's blue back.

**Scene 4 (3.6–4.8s) — REACTION:**
Close-up of Michael's face, glasses reflecting the penguins. He's biting his lip, eyes wide, barely containing himself. He looks at the camera and shakes his head slightly — "I can't believe this is real." Cut fast, don't hold.

**Scene 5 (4.8–6.0s) — PUNK ROCK:**
Cut to: A Fiordland Crested Penguin standing on a rain-soaked rock in Milford Sound. Yellow crest blowing in the wind. Mist from a waterfall behind it. Rain on the lens. The penguin stares into the distance like it's on an album cover. Michael stands in the background in rain gear, wiping his lens, leaning in for a closer look. Dramatic, moody, wild.

**Scene 6 (6.0–7.2s) — THE DIVE:**
The Fiordland Crested Penguin launches off the rock into dark teal water. Clean entry, minimal splash. Michael watches from the boat, rain on his face. The penguin resurfaces, shakes off water, looks back once — then disappears underwater. Michael nods, impressed. Camera tracks the penguin's movement.

**Scene 7 (7.2–8.4s) — THE SQUAD:**
Wide shot: 50+ Little Blue Penguins waddle ashore at Oamaru in a line at sunset. Golden-orange light on their backs. They flow past Michael's feet like a river. He sits on the pier, legs dangling, watching them pass. He doesn't move. He's part of the scene now. Camera slowly pulls back to reveal the scale.

**Scene 8 (8.4–10s) — PAYOFF:**
Michael sits on the pier, penguins between his legs, the colony flowing around him. He looks directly at camera and smiles — a real, quiet, "I can't believe this is my country" smile. Camera pulls back to reveal the full panorama: pier, colony, ocean, sunset. A moment of stillness after all the energy. This is the frame people screenshot and share.

**Technical Notes:**
- Michael's hat/cap changes per scene but his glasses remain consistent throughout
- Smooth transitions between scenes — use motion blur or whip-pan bridges
- Maintain consistent lighting progression: misty cool (Fiordland) → golden warm (Oamaru) → sunset warmth (payoff)
- Penguin behavior must look natural — no staged or artificial interactions
- Subtle lens flares acceptable on sunset shots
- No text overlays, no dialogue, no filters — clean cinematic grade only
- All locations are in the South Island of New Zealand
